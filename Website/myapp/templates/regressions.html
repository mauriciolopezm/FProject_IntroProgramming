REGRESSION RESULTS










Several regression analyses were conducted to examine a possible correlation between variations in the price of the substance and the variations in the homicide rate. As the price is a key determinant of the quantity demanded of a certain good, a higher price is likely to lead to a lower daily consumption of the substance. Hence we will be able to interpret the results as follows: A higher price in Marijuana should speak for lower consumption of the substance. So the question is whether a lower consumption increases of decreases homicide rate?

















Thereby it is diffuclt to make an absolute interpretation concerning causality, as drug consumption is addictive and thereby Marijuana is an inelastic product. What we find however is a positive correlation between the price of Marijuana and the homicide rate, indicated by the positive coefficient. We could interpret it as a one unit increase in the price of Marijuana (1$) leads to a X amount of increase in the himicide rate on a given day, ceteris paribus.
